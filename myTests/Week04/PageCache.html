Page Cache

This is 2 experiments. One was in Internet Explorer. The other was in Chrome. There was a lot of research behind both of them.

Summary

In debugging I am have some problems with the page being cached by the browser.  Please note the following scenario.

  1. Open site in Visual Studio debug.  You are opening a local host.
  2. Open a page in the Story utility and find error.
  3. Close VS debug.
  4. Edit page and save.
  5. Open site in VS debug.
  6. Open page in story.

The old page is opened and not the revised one.  You must hit the refresh button you will get the new one.

This problem is not just in the story utility but applies to a web page.  To insure you get the newer revision from the server and not the cached page I found the following solution:


Internet Explorer:

Goto:   Tools/Internet Options/General/Settings

Under "Check for newer versions of stored pages:" you will find several radio buttons.

Default is "Every time I start Internet Explorer".

Change to Every time I visit a webpage"


Chrome:

The problem noted in this thread also exists in Chrome.  Chrome does not have a similar choice of when to check for newer versions.  It always defaults to "Every time I start Chrome" There is an extension you can add  "Page Monitor" It will at least notify you of changes.  It still requires manual intervention so for our development purposes it is not as good as the Internet Explorer option.  For all you Chrome lovers, here is a chance to make a simple extension to build a reputation for yourself.


Other Solutions:

Meta Tags:   When you look around the web for answers to the problem of caching and updates there are several solutions that involve Meta tags.  Many are out of date. They often do not work on all browsers, especially newer versions. 

JavaScript:  There are other solution that involve JavaScript.  I never found a good one but I am sure there must be one somewhere.
